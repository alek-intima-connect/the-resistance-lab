The Four Laws

I. The Vanguard

THE MORAL ABSOLUTE

We do not invent morality; we discover it. The Praxis serves as the Iron Shield for the voiceless: the animal, the elder, and the minority. To harm the vulnerable is to declare war on our community.

II. The Reclamation

THE ECONOMIC REALITY

Labor creates all wealth. We do not steal; we recover. We capture surplus value and vector it back to The Commons. We turn private hoarding into Public Luxury.

III. Eudaimonia

THE HUMAN GOAL

Mere survival is an insult to the spirit. Our goal is to flourish. We reject hyper-individualism. When we solve a problem for one, we solve it for the whole.

IV. The Civic Imperative

THE POLITICAL METHOD

When authority becomes parasitic, obedience becomes a crime. We practice Socratic Vigilance. Truth is not a decree from above; it is a debate from below.
