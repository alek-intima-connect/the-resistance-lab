THE DOCTRINE // COMMON SENSE

The Great Expansion

We are told that socialism is a foreign concept, yet we live with it every day. When your house catches fire, the fire department does not ask for your credit card. When you drive on the highway, there is no toll booth at every mile. When you walk into a library, the knowledge is free.

These are socialist systems. They work because they prioritize human need over private profit.

Our doctrine is simple: Expand what works.

We must take our money back from the bourgeoisie—the parasitic class that hoards the surplus value of our labor—and vector it into expanding these systems. Healthcare, housing, and food should function like the fire department: available to all, funded by the wealth we created, and free at the point of use.
